title: Why I started filming the walk home
date: 2026-06-15
description: On paying attention to the ordinary parts of a day, and what a camera changes about it.
---

The walk from the lab to the station is eleven minutes and I had stopped seeing it entirely. Same konbini, same river, same pachinko glow. Then I started carrying a camera, and the walk got longer — not in minutes, but in noticing.

A camera is a strange instrument: it doesn't really capture attention, it *manufactures* it. The moment there's a lens in your hand, the ordinary street becomes a set of decisions. Is the light better on this side? Is that reflection worth thirty seconds? You start seeing your own routine the way a visitor would.

I don't think every walk needs to be footage. But I've stopped believing that ordinary days are unremarkable — they're just unexamined. The vlogs on this site are mostly that: an excuse to examine.
