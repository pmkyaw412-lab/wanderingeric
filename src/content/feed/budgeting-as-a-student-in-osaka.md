Every budgeting method I tried failed the same way: it survived exactly until the first irregular week. A conference, a deadline crunch, one nomikai too many — and the spreadsheet quietly stopped getting opened.

What finally worked was embarrassingly old-fashioned: envelopes. Not literal paper ones, but the idea — money divided into small named buckets at the start of the month, spent down without ceremony. Rent, lab lunches, camera fund, wander money. When a bucket is empty, that category is done until next month. No reconciliation, no guilt spiral, no abandoned spreadsheet.

The deeper lesson wasn't about money at all. Systems survive when they cost nothing to maintain on your worst week. That principle now shapes most of the small tools I build.
