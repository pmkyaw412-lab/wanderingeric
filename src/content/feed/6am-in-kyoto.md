Caught the first Keihan train out while Osaka was still asleep, and walked the Higashiyama backstreets before the shops pulled their shutters up. There's a version of Kyoto that only exists for about ninety minutes — no crowds, just delivery bikes and the sound of someone sweeping.

Shot list for this one: the empty platform, steam off a vending-machine coffee, and the long stone slope up to Kiyomizu with nobody on it.
